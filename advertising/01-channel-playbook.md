## 0. The two findings that shape everything

1. **The local Meta auction is EMPTY.** All 8 visible competitor clinics within ~15
   miles — checked one by one in the Meta Ad Library — run **zero active ads**, and so
   does Cottage Aesthetics. Meanwhile the clinic is **last of nine on Instagram reach**
   (367 followers vs up to ~21K for Worcester's Aesthetics by Sian). Paid Meta is an
   uncontested channel for the exact demographic; organic is where she's furthest
   behind (which is precisely what the Creative Studio, 03, exists to fix).
2. **Nothing gets spent until the real site is live.** The current
   www.cottageaesthetics.co.uk is a Wix "We Are Coming Soon!" shell — with template
   placeholder text still reading "info@mysite.com … San Francisco, CA". Every channel
   below leaks conversions into that page today. The CMS build + ca.cinnamons.uk (then
   the real-domain cutover) is the gating prerequisite for ring-2 spend — and it's
   already in flight.

## 1. Catchment & targeting geometry

Core towns within ~25 min of DY10 4JA: Kidderminster (57k), Stourport (21k), Bewdley
(9k), Droitwich (25k), Bromsgrove (35k), Hagley (7k), north Worcester (104k city),
Stourbridge fringe — **total ~230–280k people ≈ ~40–60k women aged 30–65**. The
affluence skew is **east/south of the clinic** (Hagley/Clent, Ombersley, Chaddesley
Corbett, Bewdley, Droitwich pockets), not Kidderminster town. Paid geo: **radius on
DY10 4JA covering ~20–25 min drive (≈10–15 miles)**, women 30–65 where targetable,
always 18+ (02 rule 10).

## 3. Paid Meta — the workhorse (mechanics)

- Ads Manager (not Boost-only); **Instant Forms or Messages objective** — on-platform
  lead capture, because facesconsent kills UTMs (live-verified) and cosmetic-domain
  pixel events are unreliable; leads get a same-day callback/text from Purdi.
- Radius targeting per §1; 18+ enforced; test the Advantage+ audience toggle from day
  one; NOT full Advantage+ Sales (needs ~50 conversions/week — far beyond this budget).
- No side-by-side before/after in ad creatives (Meta policy) — close-up single-state
  results, Cottage interiors, credential-forward creatives instead; no
  personal-attribute copy ("your wrinkles…" — 02 rule 7).
- Benchmarks (estimates, national): UK beauty CPC £0.30–0.85, CPL £12–35; a comparable
  £500/mo UK clinic case reached ~£9.45 CPL after 2 months. Kill/scale gates per
  00 §8.
- Standing competitive intel: Meta Ad Library check monthly (a Kidderminster body-filler
  clinic, JoJo Glow, started ads 6 Jul 2026 — watch whether the empty auction lasts).

## 4. Google — GBP first, Search second

- **GBP**: primary category "Medical spa"; load Services with the dedicated price
  fields; photos of The Cottage; posts (schedulable, archive after 6 months); ignore
  Q&A (feature being phased out, API dead since Nov 2025); booking link = UTM'd site
  page (custom links have zero native reporting).
- **Search**: one tight campaign, Phrase/Exact, ad groups: consultation terms,
  treatment+geo ("profhilo kidderminster/worcester", "lip filler kidderminster",
  "microneedling kidderminster", "skin booster clinic near me"), trust terms ("nurse
  led aesthetics worcestershire"). Location assets → ads in Maps. Indicative CPCs
  £1.50–2.00 (national estimates; "london" modifiers run ~3× — small-town should run
  cheaper). **No PMax** (uncontrolled placements + banned-words vertical); **no LSA**
  (unavailable for UK aesthetics).

cover the immediate village.

## 7. Directories & registers

Save Face (§2 #4 — the verified local gap) → Glowday (§2 #7) → then check JCCP
practitioner registration, ConsultingRoom, Treatwell/Fresha fit (Fresha is used by two
local competitors; note the Dr Bunny precedent — any listing must be POM-clean, 02 §6).
GBP is the directory that matters most (§4). Yell/Thomson: low priority, free listings
only.

## 8. Measurement (per 02 rule 12)

UTM dictionary (`utm_source` ∈ meta/google/gbp/nextdoor/print/groups/email;
`utm_campaign` = `yyyymm-theme-geo`); outbound-booking-click + `tel:` tap = the
conversion events, fired on OUR domain before the facesconsent handoff; per-channel QR
codes for print; a dedicated forwarding phone number for ads/GBP as poor-man's call
tracking (proper DNI at £199–249/mo is deferred — disproportionate at this budget);
weekly digest in the Adverts dashboard. Pixels/CAPI only after the consent banner
(ring 3).
