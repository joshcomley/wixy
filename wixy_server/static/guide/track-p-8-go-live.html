Track P — Purdi · 30 minutes, with Josh on the phone

Track P, chapter 8 — Go live

This is the one chapter that changes what your visitors see — everything before now has been invisible to them. It's short, it's designed to be done together with Josh on the phone, and it can be undone in minutes if anything ever looks wrong.

  1. Call Josh, and do this chapter together.

    Where However suits you both — phone, video call, sitting together. You'll each be watching your own real website's address as it changes, so it helps to have him on the line while you do.

    You know it worked when… you're both ready, with this guide open, before you touch anything below.

  2. Before changing anything, note down what your address currently points to.

    Where In the main Cloudflare dashboard (not Zero Trust this time) for your domain, go to "DNS," then "Records." Find the row for www and the row for your bare domain (shown as @) — for each, note down (or screenshot) its "Type" and "Content" columns. This is what your website currently points to — Wix's own servers — and it's your way back if you ever need it.

    You know it worked when… you have both rows noted down or screenshotted, safely, before continuing.

  3. Point your main address at your own droplet.

    Where Back in Zero Trust → "Networks" → "Tunnels" → your tunnel → "Public Hostname" → "Add a public hostname." For "Subdomain," type www; for "Domain," choose yours; under "Service," choose type HTTP and enter the address below. Cloudflare will likely warn you a record for www already exists (the one you just noted down) — choose to replace it.
    wixy:9380

    You know it worked when… the Public Hostname list shows www.yourdomain routed to wixy:9380.

  4. Do the same for your bare address (no "www").

    Where Repeat the previous step once more: "Add a public hostname," leave "Subdomain" empty this time (so it applies to your domain on its own), same Service and same address as before. Confirm the same way if it warns about replacing an existing record.

    You know it worked when… the Public Hostname list now shows two new entries — www.yourdomain and your bare domain — both routed to wixy:9380.

  5. Visit your real address, together, and watch it change.

    Where In your browser, go to your website's normal address — the one your customers already use. Refresh a few times if the old site still shows at first; this normally clears within a minute or two, occasionally a little longer.

    You know it worked when… your real address shows your Wixy site — the same one you tested in chapter 7 — and Josh confirms he sees it too, from wherever he is.

  6. If anything looks wrong, flip it back — this takes minutes, not hours.

    Where Return to the same "Public Hostname" screen from steps 3–4 and edit (or delete) the www and bare-domain entries, restoring the "Type" and "Content" values you noted down in step 2. If you're ever unsure what those were, Wix support can always remind you — your Wix account and subscription haven't been touched by anything in this guide, so it's still there as a safety net for exactly this.
    Nothing about this guide deletes anything. Every step from chapter 1 onward has been additive — new accounts, new droplet, new tunnel routes — right up until steps 3 and 4 just now. Undoing those two is the only "undo" this whole guide ever needs.

    You know it worked when… — only if you ever need this — your address shows your old site again, exactly as before.

  7. That's it. You're independent.

    What's next Nothing urgent — but three pages are worth knowing about, for whenever you need them: Appendix A is the one page you'd hand to any developer if Josh were ever unreachable; Appendix B lays out exactly what this now costs you, including when you might choose to cancel your old Wix subscription; and Appendix C shows you how to remove Josh's access, whenever — if ever — you'd like to.

    You know it worked when… your website runs entirely on your own accounts, and you know exactly where everything lives. Well done.
