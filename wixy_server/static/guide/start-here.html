Welcome — let's make this website properly yours

Right now, Cottage Aesthetics' website runs on infrastructure that belongs to Josh. That's worked well so far, but it means Josh could — in theory — ever take it away, or something could happen to him and you'd be stuck. This guide walks you through moving everything onto accounts that are entirely yours: your own hosting, your own domain settings, your own AI assistant, your own copy of the software that runs it all. When you're done, Josh becomes someone you've chosen to keep as a helper — not someone you depend on.

Nothing breaks while you're doing this. Every step in this guide happens on a brand new, separate copy of your site — your real, live website keeps running exactly as it does today until the very last section ("Go live"), and even that step can be undone in minutes if anything feels wrong. Take your time. There's no rush and no step that can't be paused and picked up again later.

What you'll end up with

By the end, four things will be true: your website will run on a small server that's rented in your name; the address people type to visit it will be managed in your Cloudflare account; the AI assistant that helps you write and edit content will run on your Anthropic account, billed to you; and the software itself will live in a copy of the code that belongs to your GitHub organisation, which you can update whenever you choose.

How long this takes

About 2–3 hours in total, but spread across whatever pace suits you — nothing here is urgent, and most chapters are a single sitting of 10–30 minutes. Each chapter tells you its own rough time at the top. You can close this guide between chapters and pick up exactly where you left off; nothing times out or needs to be done in one go.

How this guide is organised

There are two "tracks". Track J is a short list of things Josh does once, before you start — you don't need to do anything for this part, it's just useful to know it's happened. Track P is you: eight chapters, one account or step at a time, each its own sitting. Two appendices at the end are reference pages you'll come back to later, not steps to do now: what to do if Josh is ever unreachable, and how to remove his access once you're comfortable.
