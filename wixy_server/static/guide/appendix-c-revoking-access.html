Appendix C — Revoking Josh's access

This page exists on purpose: proving you can remove Josh, cleanly and completely, whenever you like, is the whole point of everything you've just built. This isn't something to do now — it's here for whenever, if ever, you want it. Doing any or all of it needs no explanation to anyone.

Josh holds three things, and only three — each one you granted yourself, earlier in this guide, and each one removable in under a minute. He has never had your passwords, your billing details, or any way to change these back once you've removed them.

  1. Remove Josh from your GitHub site repository.

    Where Open cottage-aesthetics-site, click "Settings," then "Collaborators and teams." Find joshcomley in the list and click "Remove."

    You know it worked when… he no longer appears in that repository's collaborator list.

  2. Remove Josh's email from your Cloudflare Access policy.

    Where In Cloudflare Zero Trust, go to "Access," then "Applications," and open your admin application. Edit its policy, remove Josh's email address from the allowed list (leaving just your own), and save.
    This takes effect immediately — including ending any session he might already have open. He'd need a fresh invitation from you, added back to this same list, to ever get in again.

    You know it worked when… the policy's allowed list shows only your own email address.

  3. Remove Josh from your Anthropic console, if you added him.

    Where Only relevant if you invited him in Track P, chapter 5. If you did: Anthropic Console → Settings → Members (or "People") → find his entry → remove. If you didn't, there's nothing to do here — he was never given access in the first place.

    You know it worked when… he's no longer listed, or you've confirmed he never was.

That's the whole list — three places, three removals, each independent of the others. Nothing about your site, its history, or your own accounts changes when you do any of this. And if you ever want Josh back as a helper after removing him, chapters 2, 4, and 5 show you exactly how to re-invite him — any time, on your own terms, same as the first time.
