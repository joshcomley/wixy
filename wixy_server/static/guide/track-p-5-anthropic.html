Track P — Purdi · 10 minutes

Track P, chapter 5 — Your Anthropic account and API key

Anthropic makes Claude, the AI assistant behind your site's chat panel — the one you ask to change wording, add a section, or tweak the booking page. Today it runs on Josh's account; this chapter moves it onto your own, billed to you, with a spending cap you control.

  1. Create your Anthropic account.

    Where Go to console.anthropic.com and choose "Sign up." An email address and password (or signing up with your Google account) is enough — you'll be asked for billing details before you can create a key, in the next step.
    Save this in your password manager as "Cottage Aesthetics — Anthropic account".

    You know it worked when… you're looking at the Anthropic Console, signed in.

  2. Create your API key and save it straight away.

    Where In the Console's left-hand menu, choose "API Keys," then "Create Key." Give it a name such as "Cottage Aesthetics website," and click "Create." Anthropic shows you the key exactly once — copy it immediately, before doing anything else.
    Save this in your password manager as "Cottage Aesthetics — Anthropic API key".
    If you ever navigate away before saving it, that's not a disaster — you can't see the same key again, but you can delete it and create a new one in its place, as many times as you need.

    You know it worked when… the key is saved in your password manager, starting with sk-ant-.

  3. Set a spending limit inside your Anthropic account too — belt and braces.

    Where Still in the Console, look under "Settings" or "Billing" for something named "Limits" or "Spend limits" (Anthropic's exact wording here shifts occasionally — the Console's own search box, if you see one, is a good fallback). Set a monthly limit — $40 is a sensible starting point, matching the friendly cap your website's own settings will also enforce in chapter 6.
    This is a second, independent safety net, not a duplicate: your website has its own monthly budget setting (chapter 6) that stops new conversations once it's reached, and now your Anthropic account itself has one too. Either one alone would catch a runaway bill — having both means one small mistake in either place still can't surprise you.

    You know it worked when… the limits page shows your chosen monthly figure saved.

  4. Optional: invite Josh to your Anthropic console, so he can help if you ever hit an AI-related snag.

    Where Look under "Settings" for "Members," "People," or "Team" (again, naming varies), and use its invite option with Josh's email address. If your plan doesn't offer team members, that's fine — skip this step entirely; nothing else in this guide depends on it.
    Entirely optional, and entirely reversible — Appendix C shows exactly how to remove this whenever you'd like to. Josh already has none of your account's billing details or password either way; this only ever gives him visibility into usage and logs, never control over your payment method.

    You know it worked when… Josh's email appears in your account's member list (or, if you skipped this step, simply move on — chapter 6 is next).
